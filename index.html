India-focused scam pattern library

Filter common patterns and learn what to check before trusting an opportunity.

Fee scam

Registration or security deposit before joining

Be careful if a recruiter asks for money to “confirm” your job, release an offer letter, arrange an interview, provide a laptop, or create an employee ID.

Work-from-home

Typing, CAPTCHA, review, like-and-earn, or task-based jobs

High daily income for very simple online tasks is a major warning sign, especially when they later ask for recharge, tax, wallet unlock, or withdrawal fees.

Data theft

Early demand for Aadhaar, PAN, bank details, OTP, or remote access

A genuine employer may need documents later in a formal process, but you should not share sensitive details before verifying the employer and offer.

Overseas

Fast visa, free ticket, and guaranteed foreign job after payment

Overseas hiring has strict processes. Be extra cautious if someone promises visa sponsorship quickly but asks for processing money through personal UPI or bank accounts.

Fake appointment

Offer letter without interview or from an unofficial email

Fake offer letters often copy logos and use urgent payment language. Always check the official career page, domain name, and recruiter identity independently.

Already paid money or shared data?

Act quickly. These steps are general safety guidance, not legal advice.

Immediate steps

  • Do not pay more money, even if they threaten account blocking, police action, or legal notice.
  • Save screenshots, phone numbers, UPI IDs, bank account details, emails, receipts, and chat history.
  • Contact your bank or UPI app support immediately and ask about freezing or disputing the transaction.
  • Call India’s cyber fraud helpline 1930 if money was lost online.
  • File a complaint on the official cybercrime portal.

If documents were shared

  • Watch for loan, SIM, bank, or identity misuse alerts.
  • Do not share OTPs or video KYC links with unknown people.
  • Change passwords if you shared login details or allowed screen sharing.
  • Inform family members so they do not respond to follow-up threats.
  • Consider speaking to a qualified legal professional for serious identity misuse.

Frequently asked questions

Can this website tell me with 100% certainty whether a job is fake?

No. It checks risk signs and gives safer next steps. Final verification should be done through official company websites, official government portals, trusted placement cells, banks, cybercrime authorities, or qualified professionals.

Does the website store my job offer text?

No. This version does not send or store your input anywhere. It runs locally in your browser and forgets the text when you clear the form or close the page.

Should I ever pay money to get a job?

Be extremely careful. Genuine employers usually do not ask candidates to pay registration, interview, security, laptop, document, or joining fees through personal accounts. Verify independently before paying anyone.
